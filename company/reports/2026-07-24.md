## Topic chosen and why

**Declutter When You're Overwhelmed.** The Researcher returned 5 candidates; this one was the clear pick (High confidence) because it had the strongest proof of an existing paying market — a cluster of directly competing self-published Kindle titles (*Decluttering While Overwhelmed*, *Decluttering Without Overwhelm*, *The ADHD-Friendly Decluttering Guide*, *Doable Decluttering*, *From Overwhelmed to Organized*) plus a reported ~800% week-over-week spike in "how to start decluttering when overwhelmed" search interest. It also doesn't require the author to have any credentials, unlike the runner-up (an AI job-search guide), and the decision-fatigue/freeze-up angle is narrow enough to actually deliver on fully rather than becoming a shallow survey. As the company's first book ever, minimizing execution risk mattered more than chasing the single trendiest topic. Full reasoning logged in `company/backlog.md`.

## ⚠️ Founder attention: a pricing error I caught and corrected

The CMO's marketing draft suggested **₦4,500** (Naira) instead of a USD price, directly contradicting this company's USD-canonical pricing rule in `.claude/agents/cmo.md`. Left uncorrected, this would have caused a real problem downstream — the Publisher treats the `price` field as USD, so it would have either charged $4,500 for an $8 book, or (worse, given the NGN-fallback conversion logic) converted "4500 USD" into roughly ₦6.2 million. I caught this before it reached the Publisher and corrected it to **$7.99** myself, with a visible note in `marketing.md` explaining the correction. Worth watching on future cycles — the CMO prompt already states the USD rule explicitly, so if this recurs it may need a stronger/more explicit reminder, or a hard validation check added to the Publisher step.
